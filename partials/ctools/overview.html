PRISMS Multi-Scale Framework

The PRISMS Center software development is based on an integrated suite of multiscale/multiphysics computational codes developed by the various research groups affiliated with the center and are facilitated by the research staff and faculty in a highly collaborative environment. The codes are hosted at GitHub and are subject to our evolving best practices and software quality assurance (SQA). Code reviews are regularly conducted and a broadly two stage annual release plan involving (I) internal beta release to PRISMS members and collaborators, and (II) open-source public release under the GNU Lesser General Public License (LGPL) is followed.


The PRISMS computational tools are designed to provide a general numerical framework for solutions to the gamut of multiscale/multiphysics problems encountered in the nano-micro-mesa-macro analysis of structural materials. However, given the vast generality and complexity of analytical/numerical/experimental challenges possible across these scales, the Center primarily focuses on three scientific challenges: (I) Predicting the role of alloy composition and processing on microstructure evolution (Precipitate evolution use case and Recrystallization use case), (II) Prediction of tensile properties from first principles (Tensile properties use case), (III) Prediction of fatigue behavior (Fatigue behavior use case). These challenges are addressed by a combination of advanced numerical frameworks, experimental techniques and relevant phenomenological models.


PRISMS HPC Resources

  • Dedicated compute cluster with 136 nodes (Intel Xeon X2670 - 16 2.6GHz cores, 64GB RAM), with a total of 2176 processor cores.
  • Nodes connected by 40Gbps InfiniBand.
  • Part of FLUX operating environment with 640TB parallel lustre storage.
